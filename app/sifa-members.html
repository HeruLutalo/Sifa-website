Neema Mugala - DIRECTOR

Singer and Songwriter, Neema Mugala, is the director of SIFA and has been a stronghold of the Ottawa music scene for almost thirty years. Along with SIFA, she is currently a member of the Ottawa gospel group Voices of Praise.

She regularly performs with Rwandese artist The Mighty Popo. She has contributed to several local musicians’ many CD projects, such as Senegalese artist, Elage Mbaye.

She was a member of the African influenced band Cheza, and one of the founding members of Ottawa’s popular women’s a cappella group Malaika. Over the years, her participation in these groups lead to touring regularly across, Canada, the United States, and Europe.

Since her birth in Tanzania, Neema’s upbringing was the foundation that generated her love of African music, as well as her strong sense of Tanzanian and African pride—mindfully fostered and cultivated by her father. Other strong musical influences in Neema’s life include family friend and international star, the late Miriam Makeba.

The eighth of nine children, Neema started singing with her family at a very young age, and as she grew, often assisted her father with vocal arrangements and harmonies, and performed in front of family, friends and dignitaries.

Neema also participated in choirs throughout grade school and many of these songs from her early childhood were later incorporated into SIFA’s repertoire. As the child of a diplomat, Neema travelled and was exposed to many different cultures, yet always maintained a strong connection to her Tanzanian culture.

Ultimately, Neema created SIFA in honour of her father’s memory, as an expression of her own African pride, and as an enriching opportunity for other Africans in the diaspora to connect to their African heritage.

Nokuthula Dintwa - Soprano

My name is Nokuthula Msila Dintwa and I am originally from South Africa. In 1995 I joined SIFA. I saw one of their performances at a community event, and that's when I decided that I wanted to be a part of this versatile group.

The fact that this choir was showcasing African culture through music and dance reminded me of the beauty and richness of African cultures.

Ever since joining I know that not only have I gained friends but I have also gained sisters and brothers with shared values of the strength of African cultures expressed through rhythm and dance.

I have so many favourite memorable moments. A recurring one in particular is when we sing on the Wakefield Covered Bridge for The Raging Grannies’ annual festive fund-raising day.

Their faces full of admiration and appreciation for the dynamic music are priceless. In the not too distant future, I would like to compose and arrange songs.

Elsie Abraham - Alto

I joined the group in 2004 after seeing a performance at the Black History Month Sickle Cell event in the Sandy Hill Community Centre. I was immediately drawn and smitten by the idea of singing a cappella African folk music.

It just feels completely natural to me and somehow I have been looking for this since I was born. As a child, I tried to get involved in African dance lessons at a young age but was discouraged early on by my parents in favour of Eurocentric classical ballet and Baroque music among other European artistic cultural expressions.

I soon rejected it all with impetuous determination; and instead, dreams from my African ancestors haunted me. So, for me, SIFA was a gift from the heavens which made me whole.

Being part of SIFA brings me joy and serenity; balance and sanity; it is simply inspirational, catalytic and cathartic. I can’t wait for the release of our next CD.

Irene Havyarimana - Alto

Every singing session is a new and yet different trip back to the depths and beauty of Africa or to some places only known by those who live their passion.

Salomé St-Vil - Alto

I joined SIFA choir in 2011. The first time that I heard SIFA was at the I. C. Africa fund-raising festival in July 2011. I absolutely loved it and luckily they are always looking for new members. So, soon I made the time and wrote Neema an email and the rest is history.

I decided to join SIFA because I truly enjoy singing and performing; I also wanted to challenge myself musically. I've gained a better understanding of my roots as a member of the African diaspora.

Some of my favorite memories with the group are from during practices when we have fun and interesting conversations. I would like to sing with the group, perform solos and write songs for our choir.
